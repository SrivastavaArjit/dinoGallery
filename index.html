Dinosaurs were animals that lived millions of years ago. Some were the most colossal creatures ever to have lived on land. Their skin was scaly, like repltile skin, but some dinosaurs had feathers, too. The smallest adult dinosaurs known were about the size of today's birds. All dinosaur babies were tiny when they hatched from eggs.

Triassic Period

The Triassic is the first and shortest period of the Mesozoic Era. Both the start and end of the period are marked by major extinction events. It was well into the middle of the Triassic before life recovered its former diverstiy. Archosaurs, were the chief terrestial vertebrates during this time. A specialized subgroup of archosaurs, called dinosaurs, first appeared in this era. Herrerasaurus and Coelophysis were amongst some of the common dinosaurs find in this era.

HERRERASAURUS
(he-RAIR-ah-SORE-uss)

Herrerasaurus was the oldest dinosaur to have been found so far. It lived about 228 million years ago. In height they are equal of humans but they are biggest meat-eaters in Triassic period. It ran on two legs and had a long tail to help it balance. Its long, pointed head had small, sharp teeth for gripping prey.

COELOPHYSIS
(SEE-low-FYE-siss)

Coelophysis was a fast, vicious meat-eating dinosaur of the Triassic period. It was a size of a small car. Many Coelophysis fossils were found together at Ghost Ranch, New Mexico, as if a huge catastrophe, such as a flash flood, killed them all at once. Coelophysis was at war with other Triassic reptiles. It could catch smaller ones, but it was hunted by large predators such as phytosaurs, giant crocodiles look-alikes that were as long as school buses.

Jurassic Period

The Jurassic constitutes the middle period of the Mesozoic Era and is named after the Jura Mountains, where limestone strata from the peeriod were first identified. The start of the Jurrasic was marked by the major Triassic-Jurassic extinction event, associated with the eruption of the Central Atlantic Magmatic Province. The end of the Jurassic, however, has no clear boundary with the Cretaceous and is the only boundary between geological periods to remain formally undefined. Diplodocus and Compsognathus were amongst some of the common dinosaurs find in this era.

DIPLODOCUS
(di-PLOD-oh-kuss)

Diplodocus was a member of the superhuge sauropod group. Diplodocus had an appetite as large as its body. It probably ate for up to 20 hours a day to maintain its massive body size. It is thought that Diplodocus laid its eggs while it walked! Diplodocus was about as long as two school buses. They had a long tail that it could have used to whip its enemies. It is believed that Diplodocus would have swallowed small stoned to help grind up the unchewed plants in its stomach.

COMPSOGNATHUS
(komp-sog-NAYTH-uss)

Compsognathus is one of the smallest dinosaurs ever found. It was fast, a meat-eater, and about the size of a turkey. It was a fierce little dinosaur with a small head and a mouth filled with sharp, deadly teeth. They were built for speed. It ran on two thin legs and had a light body, a long tail for balance when turning corners quickly, and a long, flexible neck.

STEGOSAURUS
(STEG-oh-SORE-uss)

Stegosaurus was a giant plant-eating dinosaur characterized by the distinctive kite-shaped upright plates along their backs and spikes on their tails. The function of this array of plates and spikes has been the subject of much speculation among scientists. Today, it is generally agreed that their spiked tails were most likely used for defense against predators, while their plates may have been used for thermoregulatory functions.

Cretaceous Period

The Cretaceous is the third and final period of the Mesozoic Era, as well as the longest. The name is derived fromt the Latin creta, "chalk", which is abundant in the latter half of the period. It was a period with a relatively warm climate, resulting in high eustatic sea levels. During the Early Cretaceous, flowering plants appeared and began to reapidly diversify, becoming the dominant group of plants across the Earth. The Cretaceous ended with the Cretaceous-Paleogene extinction event, a large mass extinction in which many groups, including non-avian dinosaurs, and large marine reptiles, died out.

EDMONTOSAURUS
(ed-MON-toh-SORE-uss)

Edmontosauruswas a massive hadrosaur, or duck-billed dinosaur. It was a plant-eater that lived in large herds in swampy areas. It was a slow-moving dinosaur, but its skull shows that it may have had good eyesight, hearing, and sesnse of smell to keep it alert for large reptiles lurking in the water. They had loose skin around its nose, which it might have blown out like a balloon as a display or to make calls.

SPINOSAURUS
(SPY-no-SORE-uss)

Spinosaurus would have been a frightening sight, with its dragon-like sail and supersharp teeth. The huge sail stood 6 feet above the back. It was supported by bony spines. It's nostrils were high up on its nose so that msot of its head could reach into water. Scientists thought that dinosaurus ate only meat or plants, until they discovered Spinosaurus- it was a meat-eater that caught and ate fish, too.

TYRANNOSAURUS
(TY-ran-no-SORE-uss)

Tyrannosaurus Mighty T-rex was a HUGE dinosaur with a giant head, powerful back legs, and short arms that could barely reach its mouth. It's bite was eight times more powerful than a lion's. T-rex had teeth with edges as sharp as knives, to cut through flesh, and as thick as bananas, to chomp through bone.

VELOCIRAPTOR
(ve-lo-CI-rap-TOR)

Velociraptor was a small but ferocious predator that probably hunted in packs. It was a feathered carnivore with a long tail and an enlarged sickle-shaped claw on each hindfoot, which is thought to have been used to tackle and tear into prey. They can be distinguished from other dinosaurs by its long and low skull, with an upturned snout. Velociraptor was roughly the size of the turkey, and is well known to paleontologists, with over a dozen described fossil skeletons.
